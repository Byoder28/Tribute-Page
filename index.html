Charles Augustus Lindbergh

The first man to fly solo across the Atlantic

This is a timeline of Charles Lindbergh's life:

  • 1902 - Born in Detroit, Michigan
  • 1905 - Family house is destroyed by fire
  • 1905-1906 - Family house is re-built but never completely finished
  • 1911 - Biplane flies past his house in Little Falls. This leads to Lindbergh imagining flying through the air around valleys and rivers
  • 1916 - Drives his mother, Uncle Charles H. Land Jr., and their dog to California to visit his step-sister. The trip was 40 days and they spent the winter in Redondo Beach before returning to Little Falls in the spring
  • 1918 - Charles leaves high school to help farm during WWI due to so many men being taken away by the draft. He returned to school to receive his diploma and then ran a dairy farm for the following two years. The same year, the armistice was signed, ruining Charles' plans of joining the Army as a pilot at the age of 18
  • 1920 - Enrolled in the University of Wisconsion and also joined the Reserve Officer Training Corps. He dropped out of college after three semesters
  • 1922 - Lindbergh joins the Nebraska Aircraft Corporation Flight School and is taken up in a Lincoln Standard Tourabout for his first flight. The company was then sold before Charles could complete his first solo flight and training. Also in this year, he completes his first parachute jump and decides that it will be his one and only
  • 1923 - Charles buys a plane and completes his first solo flight after only a short practice with another aviator
  • 1924-1925 - Travels to Brooks Field, Texas to train in the Army. He graduates second in his class and continues to Kelley Field
  • 1924 - Father dies of brain tumor in Crookston, Minnesota
  • 1925 - Completes his first emergency jump due to a midair collision from an SE-5 scout bi-plane. Successfully parachutes to safety on the ground
  • 1925 - Graduates from US Air Service Flying School at Kelly Field and becomes a second lieutenant in the Air Service Reserve Corps. From here he moves to St. Louis
  • 1926 - Becomes chief pilot for Robertson Aircraft Corporation's St. Louis to Chicago Airmail route
  • 1927 - Signs contract with Ryan Aeronautical Co. in San Diego to build an aircraft to complete the New York to Paris flight for the $25,000 prize
  • May 20, 1927 - Charles Lindbergh departs from Roosevelt Field, Long Island in the Spirit of St. Louis on his journey across the Atlantic to Paris
  • May 21, 1927 - Lands at Le Bourget airfield, Paris after 33 hours, 29 minutes, and 30 seconds. He is successfully the first person to fly nonstop and solo across the Atlantic
  • July 20 - October 23, 1927 - Tours across the US and also completes nonstop flight from Washington to Mexico City where he is awarded the Medal of Honor.
  • December 1927 - Meets the US Ambassador to Mexico's daughter, Anne, who later becomes his wife
  • 1930 - His son is born, Charles Augustus Lindbergh Jr.
  • 1932 - Son is kidnapped from their home in Hopewell, New Jersey and is murdered
  • 1935 - Charles Lindbergh and his family move to England
  • 1938 - Family moves to Illiec Island, France and this year is also awarded the "German Eagle" by Herman Goering
  • 1939 - Charles travels back to the United States
  • 1941 - He joins the America First Movement
  • 1944 - Travels to the South Pacific and flies 50 combat missions without the White House or Secretary of the Navy having knowledge of it
  • 1954 - Wins Pulitzer Prize for the Spirit of St. Louis. President Dwight D. Eisenhower gives him the rank of brigadier general in the Air Force
  • 1957 - The Spirit of St. Louis premieres in Hollywood starring Jimmy Stewart
  • 1974 - Charles Augusts Lindbergh dies from lymphatic cancer at his home in Maui, Hawaii. He was 72 years of age.
